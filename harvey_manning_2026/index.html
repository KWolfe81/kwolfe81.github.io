A Fable of the Issaquah Alps

The Story of the Wolf, the Gazelle & the Eighteen Mountain Peaks

as told by Christie Allemand

18 peaks  ·  ~90 miles  ·  ~30,000 ft of gain  ·  endless boulders

Once upon a time there lived a Wolf who was mighty, clever, and endlessly adventurous. If there was a mountain nearby, the Wolf wanted to climb it. If there wasn't a mountain nearby, he'd probably go looking for one.

Not far away lived a Gazelle who was joyful, creative, and so optimistic that even mosquitoes felt guilty biting her. She believed every impossible thing was simply a challenge waiting for enough snacks.

The two first met during the Issy Alps 50K. While everyone else was wondering whose idea it had been to run uphill for fun, the Wolf and the Gazelle discovered they shared an even stranger hobby: climbing mountains that sensible people avoided.

🐺

The Wolf

Mighty, clever, and endlessly adventurous — and not at all fond of how many hands a good scramble seems to require.

🦌

The Gazelle

Joyful, creative, and so optimistic that even mosquitoes felt guilty biting her. Every impossible thing is just a challenge waiting for enough snacks.

One day the Gazelle heard whispers of the Harvey Manning Peak Challenge — a quest involving eighteen mountain summits. Even more puzzling, no female had ever completed it.

"That doesn't seem right," said the Gazelle. "I know plenty of women tougher than mountain goats."

The Wolf nodded. "Then I suppose we'd better go prove the mountains wrong."

And so the adventure began.

McClellan Butte

Before sunrise they met their trusted goat friend, Vasanth, who possessed the greatest superpower of them all — a reliable car and the willingness to drive people to ridiculous places before dawn.

At exactly six o'clock they set off toward McClellan Butte.

The Gazelle danced happily over the summit scramble. The Wolf stared at the rocks.

"There are... entirely too many hands involved in this hiking."

"You have paws," laughed the Gazelle.

"I know what I said."

After saying farewell to Vasanth, they disappeared into forests, boulder fields, forgotten service roads, mysterious game trails, and enough bushwhacking to convince both of them that every tree in the Cascades secretly wanted a hug.

Mount Gardner climbed steeply. Little St. Helens rewarded them with a spectacular view of Mount Rainier.

"Totally worth being scratched by seventeen different bushes," declared the Gazelle.

The Wolf silently picked another twig out of his fur.

Their route toward Scout Patrol wandered through truck roads and magical forests.

"This place is enchanted," whispered the Gazelle.

"It's definitely enchanted," agreed the Wolf. "The trees keep moving."

"No..."

"They absolutely are."

Gardner · Little St. Helens · Scout

Eventually they reached Humpback, where news arrived that their friend Dave was chasing them.

"It's nice to have friends," said the Gazelle.

"It's less nice when they're faster than GPS," replied the Wolf.

Dave soon appeared, installed live tracking on the Gazelle's watch, and disappeared almost as quickly.

At Pratt Lake Trailhead more friends waited with food and supplies. Trevor examined the Gazelle's feet.

"Those are impressive blisters."

"I was hoping they'd become calluses."

"They've chosen violence instead."

Humpback · Granite · Pratt

Granite Mountain greeted them with daylight. Pratt Mountain greeted them with darkness. Bandera greeted them with more climbing. Mason Lake greeted them with an hour-long dirt nap.

The Wolf later insisted it was the finest hotel he'd ever visited. The Gazelle gave it three stars.

"Excellent views," he said. "Terrible room service."

As dawn returned they climbed Mount Defiance, Putrid Pete, and Web. Descending Web required nerves of steel. The rocks shifted beneath every step. The Gazelle stayed carefully above the Wolf.

"I'd rather not accidentally roll a boulder onto you."

"Appreciated."

"It would make the rescue report awkward."

Dirty Harry lived up to its reputation. Dirty Box was even worse. They squeezed through brush decorated with fresh goat hair.

"The goats clearly know the way," said the Gazelle.

"They also have four-wheel drive."

After climbing cracks, scrambling over boulders, and questioning several life choices, they finally reached Mailbox Peak.

Neither said anything for a while.

Sometimes surviving is conversation enough.

Friends — Trevor, Vasanth, and Christophe — arrived carrying the greatest treasures known to exhausted adventurers:

Broth.
Real food.
Encouragement.

The Wolf, who had spent much of the night unable to eat, finally managed a proper meal.

"It tastes amazing."

"It's broth."

"It's the greatest broth in recorded history."

Defiance · Web · Dirty Harry · Mailbox

Then came Green to Teneriffe. Everyone agreed this was the hardest section, and for some silly reason the French goat Christophe agreed to join. Everyone also wondered why he had agreed to it.

The route off Green included cliffs, sketchy game trails, creek beds, boulder fields, and what the Wolf officially classified as "the No Error Zone."

The Gazelle preferred calling it "character building."

The Wolf preferred not building any more character.

When Caroline appeared near Teneriffe with snacks, she instantly became everyone's favorite human. Even the mountains seemed to approve.

From Teneriffe to Mount Si the trail became kinder. The Gazelle smiled.

"I think we've done the hard part."

The Wolf looked suspiciously at the mountain.

"Don't let it hear you."

They descended to Little Si, where they slept briefly before beginning their final climb beneath the stars.

The Wolf finally felt stronger. The Gazelle slowly bounced uphill with the enthusiasm of someone who had somehow forgotten they had already climbed seventeen mountains.

One final summit remained.

Together they reached the top. There was no dramatic trumpet. No cheering crowd. Just tired smiles, scratched legs, torn clothes, aching calves, blistered feet, and the quiet satisfaction of finishing something extraordinary.

Green · Teneriffe · Si · The Last Summit

Vasanth drove them back to the trailhead while both promptly fell asleep somewhere between "Congratulations!" and "Does anyone want coffee?"

And so the Wolf completed the Harvey Manning Peak Challenge for a second time. The Gazelle became the first woman to complete it.

The mountains remained exactly where they had always been. But they would forever remember the Wolf who grumbled through every scramble...

...and the Gazelle who smiled through every mile.

Moral of the Story

"Impossible things are usually just very long adventures with good friends, stubborn determination, and an unreasonable amount of broth."

Field Notes

About the Challenge

The Harvey Manning Peak Challenge is a peak-bagging quest in the Issaquah Alps east of Seattle, designed by George Orozco. The objective is to summit eighteen specific peaks in a single continuous push — roughly 100 miles with around 40,000 feet of elevation gain — starting at McClellan Butte and finishing at Rattlesnake Mountain's Snoqualmie Point. There is no fixed route; most peaks have faint trails, some are boulder-field scrambles, and a few are pure bushwhacks.

It is named for conservationist Harvey Manning, who coined the term "Issaquah Alps" to elevate these foothills in the public eye and help protect them.
